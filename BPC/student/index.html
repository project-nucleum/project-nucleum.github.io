Introduction

Computing is a ubiquitous part of modern life. However, it is also clear that large groups of our population are not yet included in computing research, education, and jobs. As a part of Broadening Participation in Computing Plan under Rutgers-TCNJ collaborative research Project NUCLEUM, this student-focused workshop aims to provide high school students gain experience in artificial intelligence through one-day workshops at Rutgers Universtiy and The College of New Jersey (TCNJ).
IMPORTANT (As of April 26, 2022): ALL event attendees must provide proof of vaccination or negative PCR test from a test taken no more than 72 hours before the event when entering an event. Face coverings are required in all Rutgers and TCNJ buildings. The workshops may be cancelled if COVID-related on-campus activity restrictions are applied. Any last minute changes will be notified to accepted participants individually, as soon as the information become available.

Workshop for High School Students

Tentative Schedule of The Day
8:00 am - 10:00 am Travel to Rutgers University
10:00 am - 11:30 am

Session 1: Visit Rutgers University Research Labs

Students will be exposed to state-of-the-art research projects and outcomes in AI, computer graphics, simulation, and robotics. There will be discussion based activities and interaction with graduate students.
11:30 am - 1:30 pm Travel to TCNJ and Lunch
1:30 pm - 2:10 pm

Session 2: Visit TCNJ Computer Science Research Labs

Students will visit the TCNJ research lab participating in this project. They will be introduced to a selected set of research topics in AI that are related to this project.
2:10 pm - 2:30 pm Break
2:30 pm - 5:00 pm

Session 3: Hands-on session about selected topics in AI

Students will experience some basic examples of AI research topics that were introduced in Session 2. Specifically, students will be given a simplified robot navigation problem, and will be provided with software tools to see how the software can be used to solve the problem. Some easy-to-understand computer science concepts and algorithms used in the software will be explained as well.
You will participate in an on-going research activity by navigating and evacuating from virtual reality environments.
You will see how well you and your peers did in the evacuation tasks!
Time permitting, you will see how real robots can navigate in environments based on the steering policy you design.
5:00 pm - 6:00 pm Travel time to return to schools

Who is eligible to apply? This year (2022), students from Hamilton Township School District (HTSD) and Mercer County Technical Schools (MCTS) in New Jersey are invited to participate. We aim to expand the participants from other school districts in the years to come.
