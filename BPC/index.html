Summer Workshop on AI - Special Topic on Human, Crowd, Environment and Robotics

Background

Computing is a ubiquitous part of modern life. However, it is also clear that large groups of our population are not yet included in computing research, education, and jobs. As a part of Project NUCLEUM, this Broadening Participation in Computing (BPC) Plan will address this problem by using the research project outcome to provide educational experiences and tools that span various levels of the future computer science workforce pipeline. We aim to accomplish the following list of goals:
  • High school students will be exposed to machine learning through one-day workshops at The College of New Jersey (TCNJ)
  • K-12 teachers will be trained on how they can incorporate machine learning concepts in their classrooms
